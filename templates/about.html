PCEA Academy Brief History

A concise journey through our founding and growth milestones.

  • Preamble

    PCEA Umoja Academy-Nairobi, just like many organizations, started in a very humble beginning. The idea was born from the Women's Guild and Christian Education departments of the church.

  • beginning

    The academy opened its doors in January 2007 with two children, Abigael Muthoni and Fredrick Ayuku, and one teacher, Priscilla Mugo, assisted by Jane Wanjiku in charge of welfare. Both are still with the academy to date. In Term Two of 2007, the enrolment increased to 43.

  • PIONEER BOARD MEMBERS

    The first chairman was Mr. Mario. Other board members were Mrs. Maina, Mrs. Njiri, and Mr. Thirima.

    Growth between 2007–2010: The enrollment had grown to 150 children and 8 teachers. It was then the management saw a need for having a professional teacher as the head of the school.

    In Term Two of 2010, the headteacher by the name Hirams was employed after a short stint of the school under church administration.

    The school continued to grow until 1st Term of 2014 when the first head left due to other commitments. He left when the enrollment was 190 children and 13 teachers. In the second term of 2014, Mr. Stephen Akenga took over the school in an acting capacity.

    In the third term of 2014, the headteacher by the name Martin Kaimenyi Mbogori was recruited. He has seen the school grow to the current enrollment of close to 800 students. The headteacher is supported and assisted by a dean of studies from every section, led by able deputy Madam Sharon Alai.

  • STAFF ESTABLISHMENT

    • The school has seen tremendous growth from two staff during inception 13 years ago and now boasts over 50 workers.
    • The school is run by a board of management comprising both reputable professionals.
    • Being a Christian-based school, the pastoral care is overseen by the PCEA Church clergy team.

  • Education System

    Our school offers the CBC (Competency-Based Curriculum) system of education. The pre-school and daycare adopt a Montessori hybrid system of Montessori and CBC curriculum approach.

Academic Performance

Celebrating our students’ outstanding achievements over the years.

Academic Performance

The academic progress of the school has been simply fabulous right from baby class to class 8.

The school participates in highly competitive exams like Embakasi private schools cluster, common Catholic exams (East Presbyter), and KNEC exams, to name but a few.

To cite a few examples, our school was ranked position 3 out of 52 schools in the Embakasi private schools cluster exams, class 8, in 2016. Our child, Erick Omondi, was ranked position 6 out of the possible 1552 learners.

In 2017, class 8’s common Catholic exam, our school was ranked position 2 out of the possible 18 schools. Our child, Naomi Jemkemoi, emerged in position one with 452 marks out of the possible 500 marks.

The school presented 17 candidates for the pioneer KNEC certified exams in the year 2017. All the candidates produced a sterling performance, with the highest candidate achieving 442 marks out of 500. The school attained a mean score of 395 marks. Since then, the academic performance has been steady.

CO-CURRICULAR ACTIVITIES

CO-CURRICULAR ACTIVITIES

The school has not been left behind in nurturing talents.

Currently, we have vibrant activities, including a Taekwondo club, music club, art club, ballet, chess, drama, Chinese language, volleyball, table tennis, football, and a journalism club, among others.

The school participates in music and drama festivals, earning awards among our children.

Due to our sterling performance in co-curricular activities, our school was invited by Royal Media Services to participate in their notable TV children’s program “Uhuru wa Wana”, which runs on Saturdays at 9 a.m. and repeats on Sundays from 3–4 p.m.
